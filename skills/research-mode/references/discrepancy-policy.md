# Discrepancy Policy

Apply this policy when sources conflict, language-specific results diverge, or recency is unclear.

## Discrepancy Types

1. Factual conflict:
   - numbers, dates, definitions, or event outcomes differ
2. Translation drift:
   - same claim appears materially different across languages
3. Temporal conflict:
   - old and new sources disagree
4. Scope mismatch:
   - sources discuss different regions, cohorts, or periods

## Resolution Procedure

1. Isolate the exact conflict statement.
2. Run targeted follow-up searches in both languages.
3. Prioritize primary or official sources when available.
4. Validate publication/effective dates.
5. Decide:
   - resolved
   - partially resolved
   - unresolved
6. Report decision with citation markers and uncertainty level.

## Reporting Rules

- If unresolved, do not force a single conclusion.
- State both sides and explain why they differ.
- Identify what additional evidence would resolve the conflict.
- Mark language-specific discrepancies explicitly.

## Confidence Labels

- High: verified by multiple independent sources with no material conflict.
- Medium: mostly consistent with minor unresolved details.
- Low: major conflicts remain or evidence is sparse.
