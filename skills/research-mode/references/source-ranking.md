# Source Ranking

Detailed criteria for evaluating and ranking source reliability.

## Tier System

### Tier 1 — Primary / Authoritative

- Official documentation from the product or organization.
- Standards body publications (ISO, W3C, IETF, etc.).
- Government and regulatory publications.
- Primary data from the entity being researched.

**Trust level**: accept unless contradicted by newer Tier 1 source.

### Tier 2 — Expert / Vetted

- Peer-reviewed academic papers.
- Established industry research firms (Gartner, Forrester, etc.).
- Major publishers with editorial review.
- Conference proceedings from recognized venues.

**Trust level**: accept when consistent with Tier 1 or when Tier 1 is unavailable.

### Tier 3 — Professional / Curated

- Major news organizations with editorial standards.
- Industry whitepapers and technical blogs from recognized experts.
- Official company blogs and press releases.

**Trust level**: use for context and trends. Verify claims against Tier 1-2 when possible.

### Tier 4 — Community / Unvetted

- Community forums (Stack Overflow, Reddit, etc.).
- Personal blogs and social media posts.
- Wikis (Wikipedia, etc.).
- User-generated tutorials and guides.

**Trust level**: useful for discovering leads and common practices. Always verify facts independently.

## Evaluation Criteria

When ranking a specific source:

1. **Authority**: is the author/publisher recognized in this domain?
2. **Recency**: when was it published or last updated?
3. **Specificity**: does it address the exact question or a related topic?
4. **Independence**: is the source independent from parties with interest in the outcome?
5. **Corroboration**: do other independent sources confirm the same claims?

## Override Rules

A lower-tier source can override a higher-tier one when:

1. The higher-tier source is outdated and the lower-tier provides verifiable newer data.
2. The higher-tier source contains a documented error or retraction.
3. Multiple independent lower-tier sources converge on a different conclusion.

In all override cases, document the reasoning explicitly in the response.
